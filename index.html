The workshop is intended for data analytics practitioners and researchers interested in applying their methods to areas with potential ethical implications. As this year's KDD theme is raising awareness about the potential of data mining for social good, it is the perfect venue for promoting discussion of the ethical pitfalls and possibilities that often arise when working with data, especially in areas with social impact. This is a topic that is worthy of a serious conversation, yet sorely under-discussed in the data community.

Topics

This workshop addresses ethical issues in data collection, storage, analysis, and sharing, including:
  • Responsibility of data tool-makers and data analyzers to consider their work's potential ethical implications, both positive and negative
  • Intentional and unintentional impact
  • Balancing transparency/openness vs. security
  • Balancing reward and risk of data usage
  • Data use and re-use
  • Case studies of ethical issues that have arisen in data science projects
  • Hippocratic Oath for Data Scientists
  • Development of ethical norms and/or suggested checklists for data practitioners
